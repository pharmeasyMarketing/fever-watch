What changed & why

End-user view of the score breakdown. Each change maps to one of the seven open refinements flagged on 17 Jun. This panel is annotation only - it is not part of the screen.

1
Humanised readouts. Each signal now leads with a plain level (High / Favourable / Elevated) and a one-line sentence - no more "48 raw, 48%". decision lab line is qualitative + guardrail-safe; see question below.
2
"35% reference" removed from the consumer view. Users see High / Moderate / Low; the 35% (now per-fever) anchor moves to "How we calculate the score".
3
Contribution split from trend. The +N "to the score" badge (top-right) is now separate from the Rising / Easing vs yesterday chip (bottom-left), so "+30" no longer reads as "rose by 30".
4
Deltas get a timeframe. The disease list is headed "Change since yesterday"; the period tab swaps it to "vs last week" / "vs last month".
5
"Why 56, not 60?" A tap-to-open line explains the max-dominant blend (0.8 x top fever + 0.2 x the rest) right under the dial.
6
Per-fever lab scale (the calibration card). Malaria ~4%, chikungunya ~15%, dengue ~25%, typhoid ~45% replace the single 35% rule.
7
Accessibility. Trend arrows are shape-coded (▲/▼ + words, not colour alone), level text uses 800-weight on tinted pills for contrast, tap targets ≥ 44px.
